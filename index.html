EXPERIMENTS STEPS

We have many steps for this, like our steps are

Step 1

Design and Analysis of Slabs

As slabs are the initial load bearers of any residential building, we have started the work with it first. Our building plan consist of three slabs namely S1 , S2 & S3

Step 2

Design and Analysis of Beams

Beams are vital horizontal elements in reinforced concrete construction, distributing loads from slabs to columns, crucial for structural stability. Proper design, material selection, and construction.

Step 3

Design and Analysis of Columns

Columns are integral to RCC structures, providing vertical support and stability. With careful design and maintenance, they ensure structural integrity and safety.

Step 4

Design and Analysis of Footings

Footings in RCC construction serve as essential bases, distributing vertical loads and ensuring stability in the foundation system.

Step 5

Mixing of Recycled Concrete

Samples with varying proportions of recycled concrete undergo tests to determine the optimal mix for strength, durability, and occupant safety.

About Us

Welcome to our innovative project at the intersection of civil engineering and sustainability.
Our endeavor focuses on exploring and implementing advanced techniques in residential construction, specifically utilizing reinforced cement concrete (RCC).
With an emphasis on sustainability, we aim to redefine construction standards by seamlessly integrating eco-friendly materials and energy-efficient design principles into every aspect of our project.

Sustainability-Centric Approach:

Our project prioritizes sustainability, recognizing the importance of eco-friendly construction materials and energy-efficient design principles. By combining structural integrity with environmental responsibility, we strive to create not only secure living spaces but also contribute to the broader objective of sustainable urban development.


Innovative Cost Analysis:

An innovative aspect of our project is the detailed cost analysis, benchmarking our proposed RCC design against traditional construction methods. We adhere to esteemed codes like IS code 456:2000, examining material costs, construction timelines, and long-term maintenance expenses with a focus on integrating recycled concrete as a sustainable building material.


Educational Initiative:

As an educational initiative, our project serves as a crucible for students in civil engineering. It offers a holistic learning experience, seamlessly integrating theoretical concepts with practical applications. Through a deep dive into structural design principles, project management, and the creative utilization of eco-friendly materials, students gain valuable insights contributing to ongoing discourse on optimizing residential structures for safety and environmental sustainability.
